# Overview of Nutrition: Small Animals

## Summary

Domestic dogs and cats belong to the order Carnivora. Dogs have evolved to digest carbohydrates efficiently and are considered omnivores from a nutritional perspective. Cats remain obligate carnivores because several essential nutrients must originate from animal tissues.

---

# Commercial Pet Food

Producing high-quality commercial pet food is a complex process.

Pet owners often evaluate foods according to ingredient lists, but animals require nutrients rather than ingredients.

Important considerations include:

- Research supporting the diet
- Feeding trials
- Food safety
- Veterinary nutritionists
- Quality control

---

# Body Condition Score (BCS)

Body Condition Score (BCS) estimates body fat.

Two systems exist:

| Scale | Ideal |
|-------|------:|
| 5-point | 3 |
| 9-point (Dogs) | 4–5 |
| 9-point (Cats) | 5 |

---

## Very Thin

### Clinical Findings

- Ribs easily palpable
- No fat cover
- Severe abdominal tuck
- Prominent bones

---

## Ideal

### Clinical Findings

- Slight fat cover
- Waist visible
- Abdomen tucked

---

## Obese

### Clinical Findings

- Thick fat cover
- Waist absent
- Fat hanging from abdomen

---

# Obesity

Obesity is the most common nutritional disorder in companion animals.

According to the Association for Pet Obesity Prevention:

- Dogs overweight/obese: 59%
- Cats overweight/obese: 61%

---

# Muscle Condition Score (MCS)

Muscle Condition Score evaluates muscle mass independently of body fat.

It should always be assessed together with BCS.

---

# Key Takeaways

- Dogs are nutritional omnivores.
- Cats are obligate carnivores.
- BCS evaluates body fat.
- MCS evaluates muscle mass.
- Commercial diets should be complete and balanced.
